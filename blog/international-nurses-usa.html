How International Nurses Can Work in the United States

The United States continues to experience a high demand for qualified nurses due to an aging population, growing healthcare needs, and nursing shortages in many hospitals. As a result, international nurses are increasingly being recruited to work in the U.S. However, becoming a registered nurse in the United States requires completing several important steps including credential evaluation, licensing examinations, and immigration procedures.

Step 1: Verify Your Nursing Education

Before international nurses can practice in the United States, their educational credentials must be evaluated to ensure they meet U.S. standards. This process confirms that your nursing degree and clinical training are equivalent to those required for registered nurses in the United States.

Credential evaluation organizations review the following:

  • Nursing degree certificate
  • Academic transcripts
  • Clinical training hours
  • Nursing license verification

One of the most recognized organizations performing this evaluation is CGFNS International.

Step 2: Apply to a State Board of Nursing

Nursing in the United States is regulated at the state level. This means each state has its own Board of Nursing that determines licensing requirements.

Examples include:

  • Texas Board of Nursing
  • California Board of Registered Nursing
  • New York State Board of Nursing

International nurses must apply for licensure in the state where they intend to work. Some states may require additional documentation or verification processes.

Step 3: Pass the NCLEX-RN Examination

The NCLEX-RN exam is the national licensing exam required for nurses who wish to practice as Registered Nurses in the United States.

The exam evaluates a nurse's ability to apply clinical knowledge in real patient care situations. Topics commonly tested include:

  • Patient safety and infection control
  • Pharmacology and medication administration
  • Health promotion and maintenance
  • Psychosocial integrity
  • Physiological integrity

Most candidates prepare for several months before taking the NCLEX.

Step 4: Obtain Visa Sponsorship

International nurses must obtain legal authorization to work in the United States. Many nurses enter the country through employment-based immigration programs.

The most common pathway is the EB-3 immigrant visa, which allows foreign nurses to obtain permanent residency while working in the U.S.

Hospitals experiencing nursing shortages often sponsor international nurses.

Step 5: Secure Employment

Once you pass the NCLEX and receive work authorization, you can begin applying for nursing positions in hospitals and healthcare facilities.

Common workplaces include:

  • Hospitals
  • Long-term care facilities
  • Rehabilitation centers
  • Home health agencies

Benefits of Working as a Nurse in the USA

  • Competitive salaries
  • Access to advanced medical technology
  • Professional growth opportunities
  • Potential pathway to permanent residency

Although the process requires dedication and preparation, many international nurses successfully build rewarding careers in the United States.
