States That Do Not Require IELTS for Nurses

Many international nurses believe that passing the IELTS exam is always required to work in the United States. While English language proficiency is important, some state boards of nursing may waive IELTS requirements under certain conditions.

When IELTS May Be Waived

Some nursing boards may waive English testing requirements if the applicant completed their nursing education in English.

  • Nursing education conducted entirely in English
  • Country where English is the primary language of instruction
  • Official documentation confirming English instruction

Examples of States That May Waive IELTS

Policies can change, but examples of states where IELTS may sometimes be waived include:

  • New York
  • Texas
  • Illinois
  • Arizona

Applicants should always confirm requirements directly with the state board of nursing.

Important Immigration Requirement

Even if a nursing board waives IELTS, immigration authorities may still require proof of English proficiency when applying for work visas.

Alternative English Exams

Two widely accepted exams for healthcare professionals include:

  • IELTS Academic
  • OET (Occupational English Test)

The OET exam is specifically designed for healthcare workers and focuses on real medical communication scenarios.
