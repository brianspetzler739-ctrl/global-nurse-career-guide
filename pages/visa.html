Nursing Visa and Immigration Guide

Learn about visa pathways and immigration processes for nurses who want to work abroad.

Overview

International nurses must obtain appropriate work authorization before practicing abroad. Most countries facing nursing shortages provide immigration pathways for healthcare professionals.

United States Visa Options

  • EB-3 Employment Based Green Card
  • Schedule A immigrant visa for nurses
  • H-1B visa for specialized nursing roles

Canada Immigration Pathways

  • Express Entry skilled worker program
  • Provincial Nominee Programs
  • Atlantic Immigration Program

United Kingdom Visa

  • Health and Care Worker Visa
  • Requires NMC registration and employer sponsorship

Australia Visa Options

  • Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189)
  • Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190)
  • Employer Sponsored Visa (Subclass 482)

Common Documents Required

  • Passport
  • Nursing license and education transcripts
  • English language test results
  • Credential evaluation reports
  • Employment offer or sponsorship documents
  • Medical examination

Processing Time

Visa processing times vary by country but typically range between 3 months and 12 months depending on the immigration pathway.
